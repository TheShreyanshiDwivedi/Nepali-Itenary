Before You Go

Essential Logistics

🛣️

Border Crossing

Enter via Sunauli (UP) → Belahiya/Bhairahawa — the standard India-Nepal road crossing. Border opens 6 AM. Arrive early to avoid queues. Get Vehicle Transit Permit on-site.

Transit Permit: NPR 500–800
📄

Documents

Indian citizens need no visa. Carry Passport (preferred) + Aadhaar. Bring Vehicle RC, insurance, and DL. Permit is issued at the border itself.

Passport is strongly preferred over Aadhaar alone
💵

Currency

Indian ₹100 and below are accepted. ₹500 & ₹2000 are NOT accepted anywhere in Nepal. Exchange at the border — rates are fair. 1 INR ≈ 1.6 NPR.

Carry NPR + ₹100 notes only
⛽

Fuel Strategy

Fill your tank in India before crossing — Nepal petrol costs NPR 175–180/liter (more expensive). Fill up in Butwal before Pokhara. Carry a tool kit and spare tyre.

Fill tank at Butwal, not on mountain highways
📱

Connectivity

Buy a Ncell SIM at the border — NPR 400 with data, available at kiosks. Download Maps.me offline for Nepal before crossing. UPI works in Thamel cafés and hotels.

Ncell has best coverage across the valley
🛣️

Roads

Nepal's mountain roads are narrow switchbacks, not Indian highways. Average speed is 35–45 km/h. Brakes and tyres must be in excellent condition. Take it slow — the drive is scenic, not just functional.

Allow 6–8 hrs for every 200 km stretch
Day by Day

Your Itinerary

01

Border Crossing → Pokhara

Sunauli · Bhairahawa · Prithvi Highway · Lakeside

~200 km · 5–6 hrs drive
4:00 AM

Depart from India

Aim to reach Sunauli border by 6 AM when it opens. Early arrival means shorter queues. Pack vehicle RC, insurance, DL, and ID before leaving.

6–8 AM

Sunauli / Belahiya Border Crossing

Immigration + Vehicle Transit Permit. Exchange INR to NPR at border money changers — rates are fair here. Do not carry ₹500/₹2000 notes.

Vehicle permit: NPR 500–800 ~1.5–2 hrs
8–9 AM

Breakfast in Bhairahawa

Dal-bhat or roti-tarkari at a local dhaba near the bus stand. Cheap, filling, and authentically Nepali from the first meal.

Dal-bhat: NPR 150–200
9 AM–3 PM

Drive: Bhairahawa → Pokhara

Via Siddhartha Highway (scenic, river gorges) or faster Prithvi Highway. Roads are hilly with switchbacks — drive carefully. The Kali Gandaki gorge views are exceptional.

5–6 hrs Fuel up in Butwal — cheaper than Pokhara
3–4 PM

Check In — Lakeside (Baidam), Pokhara

Budget guesthouses along Lakeside street. Hotel Butterfly or Middle Path Guesthouse are reliable picks. Most have parking. Book ahead on busy weekends.

NPR 800–1,500 / night
4–7 PM

Phewa Lake — Sunset & Boat Ride

Rent a rowboat to Tal Barahi Temple — a tiny island temple on the lake. The Annapurna reflection in the water at golden hour is Pokhara's defining image. Don't skip this.

Boat: NPR 200–300/hr 2–3 hrs
7–9 PM

Dinner — Lakeside Restaurant Strip

Try Momos (Nepali dumplings), Thakali set, or Newari khaja. Jasmine Restaurant and Momos House are dependable budget picks along the lake road.

Dinner: NPR 300–500
02

Full Day Pokhara

Sarangkot Sunrise · Davis Falls · Tibetan Camp · World Peace Pagoda

Base: Pokhara Lakeside
4:30 AM

Sarangkot Hill — Himalayan Sunrise

Drive 30 min from Lakeside, then 15-min walk to the viewpoint. The Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges ignite orange at sunrise. Non-negotiable stop. Dress in layers — it is 8–12°C at dawn even in summer.

Entry: Free 2–3 hrs Carry a jacket
8–9 AM

Breakfast — Lakeside Cafés

Try sel roti (crispy rice-flour ring bread) with masala chai, or aloo tama (potato and bamboo-shoot curry). Most cafés open by 7:30 AM.

Breakfast: NPR 150–250
9:30 AM

Devi's Falls + Gupteshwor Cave

A waterfall that plunges underground through a karst system. Directly across the road: Gupteshwor Mahadev Cave, an ancient cave with a Shiva shrine and natural formations. Visit both together — they complement each other perfectly.

Falls: NPR 50 | Cave: NPR 100 1–1.5 hrs
11 AM

Bindabasini Temple

Pokhara's important Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Bhagwati. Colorful, lively, central to local religious life. Remove footwear at the entrance. 30 minutes is enough here.

Free entry 30–45 min
12–2 PM

Lunch + Tibetan Refugee Camp

Tashiling or Tashi Palkhiel Tibetan camps are 10 min from Lakeside. Watch carpet weaving and thangka painting live. Small shops sell authentic Tibetan goods at far better prices and quality than tourist shops. Try traditional Tibetan butter tea here.

Camp entry: Free Tibetan lunch at camp café: NPR 200–350
2:30–6 PM

World Peace Pagoda (Shanti Stupa)

White Japanese-built Buddhist stupa on a hilltop south of the lake. Take a boat across the lake + a 45-min uphill hike, or drive around. Panoramic views of Phewa Lake with the Annapurna backdrop. Built by Japanese Buddhist monks. Exceptionally peaceful.

Boat: NPR 200 | Entry: NPR 40 2–3 hrs
6–9 PM

Evening Market Walk + Dinner

Browse the Lakeside street for singing bowls, woolen scarves, and handmade jewelry. Bargaining is expected. Dinner at Pumpernickel Bakery (traveler favorite) or OR2K for bigger meals.

Dinner: NPR 300–500
03

Pokhara → Kathmandu

Prithvi Highway · Mugling Fish Stop · Thamel · Pashupatinath

~200 km · 6–8 hrs drive
7 AM

Early Start — Drive to Kathmandu

Via Prithvi Highway along the Trishuli River gorge — one of Nepal's most dramatic mountain drives. Road quality is variable. Carry motion-sickness medicine. Gorge views are breathtaking along the way.

6–8 hrs drive Stop at Mugling for lunch
12–1 PM

Lunch Stop — Mugling (Riverside)

Mugling at the river confluence is famous for fresh river fish — asla (snow trout) or mahaseer from the Trishuli. Many open-air riverside restaurants. This is a highway ritual for Nepalis — the real local experience on this route.

Fish thali: NPR 300–450
3–4 PM

Arrive Kathmandu — Check In, Thamel

Thamel is the backpacker hub — close to everything. Hotel Potala or Kathmandu Peace Guest House are reliable budget picks. Street parking is tight; ask the hotel for directions to their lot before arriving.

Budget hotel: NPR 900–1,800/night
5–8 PM

Pashupatinath Temple — Evening Aarti

One of the holiest Shiva temples in the world (UNESCO World Heritage). Hindu visitors enter the main sanctum free. Others observe from the Bagmati river ghats. The evening aarti and cremation rituals are profound and sobering. Sadhus line the ghats. Do not photograph cremations or grieving families — this is a live funeral site, not a tourist attraction.

Free for Hindus Non-Hindus: NPR 1,000 2–3 hrs
8–9 PM

Dinner — Thamel

Try Newari khaja set (beaten rice, soybeans, egg, pickles, bara lentil pancake) — Kathmandu's indigenous cuisine. Thamel Kitchen or Rosemary Kitchen are solid budget options. This is the one meal you must try in the city.

Dinner: NPR 250–450
04

Kathmandu Valley — UNESCO Circuit

Swayambhunath · Boudhanath · Patan Durbar Square · Asan Bazaar

Base: Kathmandu · ~50 km local driving
7–9 AM

Swayambhunath — the Monkey Temple

Ancient Buddhist stupa on a forested hill west of Kathmandu. Climb 365 steps (one per day of the year) for a panoramic valley view and resident rhesus monkeys. The all-seeing Buddha eyes on the stupa look in four cardinal directions. Spin prayer wheels clockwise along the path. Arrive early to beat crowds.

Entry: NPR 200 1.5–2 hrs
9:30 AM

Breakfast Near Thamel

Aloo paratha with chai, or egg bhurji Nepali-style (with tomatoes and green chilies). Quick stop before the day's main sights.

Breakfast: NPR 120–200
10:30 AM

Boudhanath Stupa

One of the largest stupas in the world — a massive mandala rising from the valley floor, encircled by Tibetan monasteries and prayer flags. Walk the kora (circumambulation path) clockwise with locals. Prayer wheels line the entire base. Rooftop monastery cafés offer panoramic views and cheap meals. Tibetan culture is strongest here.

Entry: NPR 400 1.5–2 hrs
1 PM

Rooftop Lunch at Boudhanath

Several rooftop cafés overlook the stupa. Try Tibetan thukpa (noodle soup) or momo soup. One of the best lunch settings in all of Nepal.

Lunch: NPR 250–400
2:30 PM

Patan (Lalitpur) Durbar Square

The best-preserved Durbar Square in the valley. Medieval Newari architecture: Krishna Mandir (all-stone temple), Hiranya Varna Mahavihar (Golden Temple), and the Patan Museum inside the old royal palace. UNESCO World Heritage. The most architecturally refined spot on this trip.

Entry: NPR 1,000 2–2.5 hrs
5:30 PM

Asan Bazaar & Indra Chowk

Kathmandu's oldest living market — dense lanes of spice sellers, vegetable vendors, bead shops, and thangka dealers. Indra Chowk has the famous bead market: glass beads, coral, and turquoise. Street food: chatpate (Nepali spicy puffed rice). Very local, not touristy.

Entry: Free 1.5–2 hrs
05

Bhaktapur + Shopping + Return Drive

Bhaktapur Durbar · Thamel Market · Sunauli Border

~310 km to border · 7–8 hrs drive
7–10 AM

Bhaktapur Durbar Square

The most intact medieval Newari city — the 55-Window Palace, Nyatapola Temple (5-story pagoda), and Potters' Square where traditional pottery is still made and sold. Also famous for Juju Dhau (king curd) — thick clay-pot yogurt that is legendary. More authentic than Kathmandu's squares. One of the best-preserved medieval cities in Asia.

Entry: NPR 1,500 (includes museum) 2.5–3 hrs Juju Dhau: NPR 80–120
11 AM–1 PM

Thamel — Final Shopping Round

Budget 2 hours. Best buys: singing bowls (bargain hard), Pashmina shawls (burn test — real wool burns clean, synthetic melts), thangka paintings (verify handmade), Lokta paper notebooks, Khukuri knives, Ilam tea, copper statues. Bargain to 50–60% of the first quoted price.

Bargain to 50–60% of asking price
1 PM — Depart

Drive: Kathmandu → Sunauli Border

~310 km via Prithvi Highway → Butwal → Sunauli. Allow 7–8 hrs. Leaving by 1 PM gets you to the border before it closes at 10 PM. Fuel stop in Butwal. Nepal-side border crossing is simpler than entry.

7–8 hrs drive Fill fuel in Nepal before crossing — cheaper
What to Eat

Food Guide

Must try

Dal-Bhat दाल भात

Nepal's national meal — lentil soup, rice, vegetable curry, and pickles. Unlimited refills everywhere. The best-value meal in the country. Eaten twice daily by most Nepalis.

NPR 150–250 · Refills included
Iconic

Momo म:म:

Nepal-Tibetan dumplings — steamed or fried, with buff (buffalo), chicken, or vegetable filling. Served with spicy tomato achaar. Buff momo is the local classic.

NPR 120–200 / plate of 10
Kathmandu

Newari Khaja Set

Chiura (beaten rice), black-eyed beans, fried egg, spiced meat, pickles, and bara (lentil pancake). Kathmandu's indigenous platter. Eat it in the old city bazaar lanes.

NPR 200–350
Tibetan

Thukpa

Hand-pulled or flat noodles in clear broth with vegetables or meat. Warming and hearty. Perfect after a cold dawn at Sarangkot or a morning at Boudhanath.

NPR 150–280
Breakfast

Sel Roti + Chiya

Crispy ring-shaped rice flour bread, deep fried. Classic morning street snack. Pair with spiced Nepali black tea (chiya). Sold from street carts from 6 AM onwards.

NPR 20–40 per piece
Bhaktapur only

Juju Dhau King Curd

Thick, slightly sweet yogurt made in unglazed clay pots — a Bhaktapur exclusive. Only authentic when eaten fresh in Bhaktapur itself. Do not attempt to carry it home.

NPR 80–120 per pot
Highway

River Fish — Mugling

Fresh asla (snow trout) or mahaseer from the Trishuli River. Fried or curried. Only available at riverside dhabas on the Prithvi Highway. A Nepali highway ritual.

NPR 300–500
Drink

Tongba + Raksi

Tongba is hot fermented millet beer in a bamboo vessel — pour hot water over it and sip through a bamboo straw. Raksi is the stronger millet spirit. Both are distinctly Nepali.

Tongba NPR 150–250 · Raksi NPR 80–150

Safe Eating Rules

💧Always drink bottled or filtered water — tap water is not safe
🍳Freshly cooked street food is safer than raw salads or pre-cut fruit
🏪Buy curd only from established restaurants, not roadside carts
🩺Carry ORS sachets and basic stomach medicine just in case

Respect & Connect

Culture & Etiquette

At Temples & Sacred Sites

👟

Always remove footwear

Applies to Hindu and Buddhist temples alike. Carry a small bag for your shoes. Socks make this easier at multiple sites per day.

↻

Circumambulate clockwise

Always walk around a stupa or mani wall with your right shoulder toward it. Spin prayer wheels clockwise. Counter-clockwise is inauspicious.

👜

No leather at some temples

The cow is sacred. Some temples (including Pashupatinath) prohibit leather goods — belt, bag, shoes. Check at the entrance. Carry a cloth bag as backup.

📵

Do not photograph cremations

At Pashupatinath's Bagmati ghats, active cremations and mourning families are present. Photographing them is deeply disrespectful. Observe in silence.

👗

Dress modestly at religious sites

Cover shoulders and knees. Shorts and sleeveless tops are fine on the street but not at temples. Many places lend sarongs at the gate for NPR 20–50.

Social Customs

🙏

Namaste — the universal greeting

Palms pressed together, a slight bow. Works everywhere. Nepalis warm immediately to Indians who use it. Avoid handshakes unless initiated by the other person.

👉

Use your right hand

For eating, passing items, and giving money. The left hand is considered impure. This applies when paying vendors and handing gifts to locals.

🐄

The cow is sacred — treat it that way

Cows roam freely in Kathmandu streets. Never honk at, touch, or disrespect them. Beef is absent from most local menus. Do not ask for it.

🏔️

Nepal is proudly never-colonized

Nepalis carry enormous cultural pride. Avoid comparing Nepal unfavorably to India. Engage with genuine curiosity. Being Indian grants no hierarchy here.

🌸

Seasons & festivals

Ghode Jatra (March), Nepali New Year Baisakh 1 (mid-April), Chaitra Dasain are major events. Book ahead if your trip overlaps — accommodation fills fast and roads get crowded.

Money Tips

  • Carry Indian ₹100 notes — accepted widely. ₹500 and ₹2000 are not accepted anywhere.
  • ATMs are available in Thamel, Lakeside, and Bhaktapur. Always carry backup cash.
  • UPI is increasingly accepted at Thamel hotels and cafés — not in local markets.
  • Tipping NPR 50–100 for waitstaff and guides is generous and appreciated.
Before You Leave

What to Pack

📄 Documents

  • Passport (preferred over Aadhaar alone)
  • Aadhaar card as backup
  • Vehicle RC + insurance papers
  • Driver's license (Indian)
  • Hotel booking printouts
  • Emergency contacts written on paper

👗 Clothing

  • Warm jacket or fleece (Sarangkot is 8–12°C at dawn)
  • Full-length pants for all temple visits
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Light rain layer (April–May showers)
  • Socks — you will remove shoes often
  • Sunglasses + hat (high-altitude UV is intense)

💊 Health

  • Motion sickness pills — roads are genuinely winding
  • ORS sachets for traveler's stomach insurance
  • High-SPF sunscreen — UV is much stronger above 1,000m
  • Personal medicines + basic first-aid kit
  • Hand sanitizer and wet wipes
  • Water purification tablets as backup

📱 Tech

  • Power bank (load shedding still occurs)
  • Universal adapter (Nepal uses Type C/D/M plugs)
  • Dashcam and spare car charger
  • Maps.me offline map downloaded for Nepal
  • Ncell SIM — buy at border kiosk (NPR ~400)
  • Cash in ₹100 notes + NPR

🚗 Car Kit

  • Fill fuel tank in India before crossing
  • Tow rope and basic tool kit
  • Spare tyre in good condition — essential
  • Emergency triangle and reflectors
  • Extra engine oil (mountain roads are hard on engines)
  • Vehicle Transit Permit (obtained at border)

✨ Nice to Have

  • Reusable cloth bag for temple shoe removal
  • Small day backpack for sightseeing days
  • Earplugs — Kathmandu street noise at night
  • Camera with good zoom for mountain shots
  • Nepali phrasebook or Google Translate offline
  • Sunscreen lip balm — easy to forget, painful to miss
